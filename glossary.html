Glossary

The following terms are used in this documentation:

access control

The combination of authentication and authorization used to control secure access to a system. In Backbase Platform, the Access Control capability provides fine-grained authorization to Digital Banking Services.

access group

A record of specific data that defines what tasks a user having a certain profile can perform; a collection of permissions and/or privileges.

access token

A token that can be provided as part of an HTTP request that grants access to the service being invoked on. This is part of the OpenID Connect and OAuth 2.0 specification.

account

An arrangement between a bank and a customer that allows the customer to pay in and take out money. A record showing a particular part of the finances of a customer.

account rebalancing

The process of realigning the weightings of one’s assets portfolio or distribution over saving goals. Rebalancing involves periodically buying or selling assets in the portfolio to maintain the original desired level of asset allocation. Rebalancing is used in the same manner in the context of saving goals.

account sweeping

The process of automatically managing funds between a set of cash and investment accounts according to predefined rules, in order to optimize the capital yields.

accounts payable

Funds owed by a company to suppliers or other creditors for goods and services purchased.

accounts receivable

Funds owed to a company by customers and other debtors for goods and services the company has provided.

accrue

Accumulate or receive in regular or increasing amounts over a period of time; applies to interests and costs.

accrued interest

Interest accumulated on a security, a deposit, or a loan between interest payment dates.

account group

A grouping mechanism for arrangements that manages access to these arrangements. Account groups contain one or more arrangements owned by participating legal entities that share arrangements.

action recipe specification

A blueprint created by developers for an action. An action recipe specification defines the components a future action must support.

action recipe

A "recipe" of an action created within the available action recipe specification; an instance of an implemented action recipe specification. For example, a specification enables a bank user to set email, sms, and in-app notifications for low account balance. A recipe created within such specification is the particular setting made by the bank user - for example, to get email notifications when their account balance is below $1000.

activity

A business process that is performed, such as “generate a report.” Activities appear on a BPMN diagram as a rectangle with rounded corners. Within activities there can also be tasks and a variety of sub-processes.

activity life cycle

The steps that need to be performed in order to execute a specific task; may be subject to additional constraints. For example, a domestic payment of 100 EUR must go through the following steps to be processed:

  1. Entered

  2. Verified by another user

  3. Authorized by another user

actual balance

See booked balance.

additions

A group in the JSON object that returns extension fields. You use data model extensions when your project needs to store information that DBS capabilities do not support out of the box.

allowed account

See designated account.

amortization schedule

See redemption scheme.

Angular app

An app which is built, tested, and developed using the Angular-CLI, and packaged as a Backbase Provisioning Package (BPP) using Backbase tooling.

Angular repository

A repository hosting source code, written in Typescript, for library packages and Angular apps, generated by Backbase Schematics.

Angular Package Format (APF)

The structure and format of the Angular framework packages currently available on npm as defined by the Angular docs.

app container

A container generated when packaging your Angular app, which holds the built Javascript and HTML for rendering the Angular app in Experience Manager.

approval

The process of a user giving consent to an intended action, which is mostly initiated by another user; the process of approving the data that is fed to the system. For specific data in a financial system, more than one approval might be required.

approval policy

A rule applied to define an approval workflow. An approval policy determines the number of users that are required to approve a request, and the profile or role of those users. For a simple approval policy, an item only requires one approval in order to move to the Approved state. For example, when a clerk approves a SEPA CT payment. A more complex approval policy would require approval from 3 users before an item is fully approved. For example, a high value payment that needs to be approved by a clerk, a manager, and the CFO.

approval level

A property associated with a user role or profile that is included in the approval policy that defines a customer’s approval workflow. For instance, an approval policy may require approvals from one or more users with different approval levels. For example, by a clerk or a manager.

arrangement

An instance of a product that is supplied by the bank and used by the customer; must be agreed by some kind of contract between the two.

arrear

Overdue amounts owed by the debtor.

arrear management

The way that a bank handles the overdue amounts owed by the debtor.

asset

Any resource of value, whether tangible or intangible, owned or used by a company to generate revenue or benefit in some other way.

asset allocation

An investment strategy that aims to balance risk and reward by apportioning a portfolio’s assets according to an individual’s goals, risk tolerance, and investment horizon. Asset allocation can be viewed as distributing the investments over different categories, such as classes, industries, countries, regions, and so on.

assign permissions

Link users to a job role so they assume the business function privileges assigned to such job role. To specify the set of arrangements that permissions apply to, an individual user is additionally linked to an account group.

authentication

The process of verifying that the user accessing Backbase Platform is who they say they are. Some parts of Backbase Platform are accessible for anonymous users, meaning the authentication process is skipped.

authentication flows

A work flow that a user must perform when interacting with certain aspects of the system. For example, a login flow can define what credential types are required. A registration flow defines what profile information a user must enter and whether something like reCAPTCHA must be used to filter out bots. Credential reset flow defines what actions a user must perform before they can reset their password.

authentication provider

The system that performs the authentication of the user. Backbase offers Identity Services as authentication provider. However, you can integrate with an external authentication provider.

authorization

The process of granting the user, either authenticated or anonymous, access to resources based on their identity. This includes verifying that the user is permitted to view or use a resource.

authorization matrix

The model that describes the tasks that a user with a specific user profile can perform.

automatic savings plan

A set of rules that manages how much of the influx on an account needs to be allocated to a savings account and how this amount should be spread over the defined saving goals.

autopay

Supports the scheduling of automatic payments of eBills using bill pay.

available balance

The amount of money that can be spent from an account; actual balance after taking into account any agreed limits as well as blocked or reserved amounts (for example, for uncleared checks).

Backbase item

An item in the Enterprise Catalog in Experience Manager, usually a container or a widget.

Backbase Provisioning Package (BPP)

A .zip file that can be imported into an experience to deploy Backbase items. On the frontend it is mainly used to deploy items to the Enterprise Catalog.

bank

An umbrella term used in the Backbase context to refer to all kinds of financial institutions, companies, and organizations that provide financial services.

bank credit

An arrangement with a bank for a loan or bank’s lending in general.

batch payment

A financial transaction that is part of a group to be processed together, sometimes also referred to as a bulk payment. However, some banks differentiate between bulk and batch, using one of the terms in relation to importing a file and the other one in relation to manually entering a group of payments, without consistency across banks.

BIC (Business Identifier Code)

A code that uniquely identifies a bank and that is used and managed by Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication (SWIFT).

bill

See invoice.

Bill Pay

A capability that provides a set of widgets and integration APIs to bill pay providers. Payveris integration comes out of the box.

bill pay

A US payment system that allows consumers to pay a person or business. Payments can be made online electronically, or by paper check. An example bill pay provider is Payveris.

blocking

The process of preventing the transfer of a specified amount of funds or securities, usually related to a specific event.

BOM

A list of node module dependencies and their versions. For example, on the frontend: package-lock, npm shrinkwrap, or yarn lock.

book date

The day when an amount is posted to the balance of an account.

booked balance

The total amount of funds that is posted to an account.

booking

See posting.

budget

An allocated amount of expenditure for a defined period of time.

bulk payment

See batch payment.

Business activity monitoring

Software that aids in the monitoring of activities that are usually implemented by computers, such as business activities, key performance indicators, business and operational exceptions, and business risks.

business function

A work activity regularly carried out by a bank or a customer through their users, such as audit, transactions, bill payments, etc.

business function privilege

A description of a task that has not yet been assigned to a user; for example, managing contacts or performing intracompany payments.

Business Process Modelling Notation (BPMN)

A graphical representation and standard for modelling business processes in a model that is readable by humans and executable by software. See https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Business_Process_Model_and_Notation to learn more.

camunda

An open-source process engine supporting BPMN. See http://camunda.org/ for more information.

card blocking

The process of temporarily changing the status of an active bank card so that it can no longer be used as a means of payment; typically done upon request of either the customer using the card or the issuing bank in cases of fraud detection.

card cancellation

The process of permanently changing the status of an active bank card so that it can no longer be used as a means of payment; typically done as part of an agreement termination upon the request of either the customer using the card or the issuing bank.

case

The collection of all information concerning a certain concept such as a request for a loan from Mr. Smith.

case data

An instance of a case data definition. Case data can exist without a process.

case data definition

Defines the structure of a certain concept such as loan origination.

case data store

Services for storing or exposing the case data.

case management

This term has two meanings:

  • A Flow capability that provides a set of case management widgets that are part of the Employee Widget Collection and that are used by back-office employees to work cases.

  • A general concept, related to the concept of dynamic case management, of coordinating work between many people on behalf of an individual who may be considered a case, often used in banking, health care, and law. The case management pattern can be implemented as software using either BPMN or CMMN standards.

Case Management Model and Notation (CMMN)

A graphical representation and standard for modelling case management processes in a model that is readable by humans and executable by software. See https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CMMN to learn more.

cash account

See current account.

cash concentration

See notional cash pooling.

charge account

An account that is connected to another account or financial product and used for posting fees and charges related to the financial product or transactions on the financial product.

check

A written order on a specially printed form directing a bank to pay a stated amount from the drawer’s (orderer’s) account to the beneficiary.

checking account

See current account.

client

An entity that can request Auth Server to authenticate a user. Most often, clients are applications and services that want to use Auth Server to secure themselves and provide a single sign-on solution. Clients can also be entities that request identity information or an access token so that they can securely invoke other services on the network that are secured by Auth Server.

client adapter

A plugin installed into an application environment to be able to communicate and be secured by Auth Server. Auth Server has a number of downloadable adapters for different platforms. There are also third-party adapters available for environments that are not covered.

client scope

A concept provided by Auth Server. When a client is registered, protocol mappers and role scope mappings must be defined for that client. It is often useful to store a client scope to make creating new clients easier by sharing some common settings. This is also useful for requesting some claims or roles to be conditionally based on the value of the scope parameter.

client role

A role namespace dedicated to the client; clients can define roles that are specific to them.

close date

The date an agreement, contract, or arrangement ends during its life. This is typically not a date that was agreed upon when the agreement was made; otherwise, it would be referred to as a maturity date.

closing balance

The balance of an account at the end of an accounting period.

collateral

An asset pledged to a bank by a customer that can be claimed in case of default.

collection

A financial transaction in which a third party withdraws funds from another party’s bank account. Usually related to bill payments on agreed dates, but can also refer to handling of checks.

collection package

a package that depends on all the related feature packages, and re-exports the APIs of the inner packages. Also known as a barrel package.

component

An Angular component.

composite role

A role that can be associated with other roles. For example, a superuser composite role could be associated with the sales-admin and order-entry-admin roles. If a user is mapped to the superuser role, they also inherit the sales-admin and order-entry-admin roles.

confirmation

The process whereby a user indicates that they agree with the task they just performed being executed.

container

A graphical element of a page in an experience that can contain other containers or widgets as children. You can use containers to build the layout of your app or to implement advanced presentation logic.

content slot

Certain areas in widgets where customers can include their own custom content coming from Customer Experience Services Content Services.

context

In Access Control, context is the service agreement that the user is working in during a session. Users only have to select the context if they work with multiple service agreements. When the context is selected, the corresponding permissions of the user are evaluated and enforced. As a result, users can access only the allowed resources and perform only their assigned business functions.

consent

In the context of Identity Services, a permission that an admin wants a user to give to a client before that client can participate in the authentication process. After a user provides their credentials, Auth Server pops up a screen identifying the client requesting a login and what identity information is requested of the user. The user can decide whether or not to grant the permission.

container component

An Angular component that is child of a widget component, and has services injected.

core banking system

The software that is used to support a bank’s most common transactions. Most core banking systems are only geared towards a particular type of banking, creating silos across the bank’s offering and creating hurdles in the digital transformation process.

correspondent bank

Bank acting on behalf of the sending bank. For example for processing electronic payments, the sending bank may use an automated clearing house. This is a financial institution that is able to process large volumes of electronic payment orders in batches. See also intermediary bank.

counterparty

The other party that participates in a financial transaction. Every transaction must have a counterparty in order for the transaction to go through. More specifically, every buyer of an asset must be paired up with a seller who is willing to sell and vice versa.

All trades require some sort of counterparty, so for example, the counterparty to an option buyer would be an option writer.

credentials

Pieces of data that are used to verify the identity of a user. Some examples are passwords, one-time passwords, digital certificates, or even fingerprints.

credit

A deposit of funds to an account, a positive accounting entry. The opposite of debit.

credit balance

A balance showing that more money has been received or is owed to a company than has been paid out or is owed by the company.

credit card

Plastic or virtual card issued by a bank that customers use to buy goods and services on credit. The issuer actually pays for the product and the customer repays the issuer later. Interest begins to accrue after a specified interval on the outstanding amount to be repaid.

credit facility

See credit limit.

credit limit

The maximum amount of credit that a bank extends to an account or a debtor.

credit line

See credit limit.

credit posting

A booking against an account that increases the balance of the account from the perspective of the bank customer.

current account

A financial product of a bank where the customer keeps a balance, which is used mainly to handle day-to-day transactions. Usually a non-interest-bearing account.

customer dialog

An interaction moment between bank and customer. Often part of a customer journey.

customer facing form

A type of customer dialogue that aims for the best possible user experience.

customer journey

A collection of customer dialogues between a bank and a customer over time that often include customer facing forms. By definition a long-running process.

customer onboarding

The process that handles addressing all prerequisites from the compliance perspective when accepting a new customer or extending a service or product portfolio for an existing one.

Customer Experience Services (CXS)

All of the essential services required to create, manage, and orchestrate end-user experiences.

data group

A group used in Access Control to give access to a set of resources. An example of a data group is an account group that can be accessed using a specific job role.

data module package

A library package that is automatically generated from RAML using Backbase Schematics. It provides a Typescript API for connecting to the REST API defined in the RAML.

day count convention

An agreement on the number of days in a month and a year used to calculate interest on a financial product. The day count convention may specify the following:

  • Number of days in a month:

    • Actual - actual number of days of the respective calendar month

    • 30 - 30 days for each month

  • Number of days in a year:

    • Actual - actual number of days in the respective calendar year

    • 360 - 360 days for each year

    • 364 - 364 days for each year

    • 365 - 365 days for each year

Based on this, the day count convention for a specific calculation can be 30/360, actual/actual, 30/actual, etc. However, not all combinations of days in a month and days in a year are commonly used, and even the same combination might not be used in calculations in exactly the same way.

debit

A withdrawal of funds from an account, a negative accounting entry.

debit balance

A balance showing that more money has been paid out or is owed by a company than has been received by or is owed to the company.

debit card

A plastic or virtual card issued by a bank that allows a customer to immediately withdraw funds from their related current account, either to make a purchase or to get cash. As opposed to a credit card, a debit card balance is normally settled immediately, though some companies allow a certain time limit.

debit posting

A booking against an account that decreases the balance of the account from the perspective of the bank customer.

decision management

A Flow capability that provides an open-source DMN-supporting rules engine and that is exposed via a Backbase service.

Decision Model Notation (DMN)

A graphical representation and standard for modelling decisions in a model that is readable by humans and executable by software. See https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decision_Model_and_Notation for more information.

default

A failure to fulfil an obligation, especially in the context of repaying a debt.

Web Design System

A collection of guidelines and tools for design and development teams to create accessible, best-practice, and aligned user experiences. While optimized for the Backbase Widget Collections (Mobile and Web), the Web Design System also provides a flexible foundation of reusable UI components, made up of Atoms, Molecules, and Widgets, guidelines, and examples for building custom widgets from scratch.

designated account

A counterpart account connected to a financial product with which financial interaction is allowed.

developer built forms

Hand-crafted forms based on building blocks.

developer persona

One of the four Flow personas; a developer is interested in programmatically working with the Flow components, especially the Process Engine, Decision Engine, and Service Integration.

Digital Banking Services (DBS)

A suite of services that consume information and facilitate communication between core banking systems and web and mobile apps. Digital Banking Services enrich and aggregate data, enhance performance, and can add additional digital banking capabilities that may not be possible if running directly from existing systems.

direct debit

A direct debit is a form of collection where a bank account holder authorizes a third party to withdraw money from their bank account. Countries and regions handle direct debits differently (find more countries listed on Wikipedia):

  • Eurozone: SEPA Direct Debit

  • United Kingdom: Direct Debit

  • United States: Direct Payment or ACH Debit

Related terms are: recurring payment and standing order.

direct grant

A way for a client to obtain an access token via a REST invocation.

direct withdrawal

See collection.

due date

The date by which a specific event or financial transaction should take place.

dynamic case management

A general concept of coordinating work between many people on behalf of an individual who may be considered a case. This is related to the general concept of case management.

eBill

An electronic version of a paper bill. Financial institutions use bill pay to enable consumers to view and pay eBills online.

entitlements

The permissions of a user on the tasks they are allowed to execute, the applicable limits, and the resources, such as data, they are allowed to access while performing that task.

entry

The process of feeding data to the system manually or automatically, for example, creating a new contact or a new international transfer.

expiration date

The date after which something, such as a credit card or an agreement, is no longer in effect.

extension slot

custom template areas where you can override certain parts of widgets, and inject your own custom templates through your Angular app.

event

An audit stream that admins can view and hook into.

experience

A website or a mobile app that provides a single point of entry to content and applications. An experience contains pages, containers, widgets, and navigation. It may also contain images and campaign and targeting elements.

Experience Manager

A web interface with which you as a content manager, web designer or Customer Experience Services administrator manage experiences for your web and mobile customers. You use Experience Manager to create and manage all aspects of the experience. You can manage pages, the content on pages, targeting, campaigns, import and export items, manage the entire experience and manage its users and permissions.

fallback template

A notification template that is used as a generic backup option by Actions when no custom template is found for a specific notification.

fintech providers

Leading digital system integrators that help our customers unlock the full potential of their investment in Backbase. Customers can maximize their technology investment by connecting with these Backbase partners who design, integrate, and deliver Backbase products and solutions around the globe.

feature package

A package that contains APIs for a single targeted feature ss opposed to a collection package. For example, a widget, a container, or a shared module.

flow node

The connection between two BPMN elements presented as an line and arrow to indicate the direction of flow.

forex trade

A deal or contract on the currency marketplace to exchange currencies. There are several ways of trading possible:

  • Spot market - deals with trading at the current price.

  • Forward market - deals with a future date of settlement against a future price (mainly OTC or Over The Counter).

  • Futures market - deals with a future date of settlement against a future price (mainly highly standardised contracts).

Foundation Angular

@backbase/foundation-ang is an underlying collection that is a dependency for all Backbase widget collections. It includes common libraries, layouts, and components.

four eyes principle

A requirement that two individuals are involved in an action before it can be taken. Banks and their customers use this to enforce the segregation of duties, to prevent both mistakes and fraud.

fulfillment provider

An existing system in a bank or a third-party service provider that handles and manages the life-cycle of a product, including payments, loan repayment, and other banking products and services.

function group

See job role.

funds transfer

The act of moving money from one account to another account. In some cases, a transfer is considered to represent a money movement within the bank.

fx trade

See forex trade.

gateway

Separates and recombines process flows. See https://camunda.com/bpmn/reference/ to learn more about the different kinds of gateways.

group

A concept designed to simplify management of groups of users. A group can have defined attributes and mapped roles. Thus, users that become members of a group inherit the attributes and role mappings of the corresponding group.

Hybrid SDK

A collection of various tools that provide a solid foundation for creating hybrid mobile applications for iOS and Android using web technologies.

identification

The process of establishing as being a particular person or thing.

Identity Services

A unified identity services solution that provides intelligent access for customers, employees, and partners, so they can securely connect to all digital and mobile banking applications and APIs. Identity Services help to prevent security breaches, manage sensitive data, and improve user engagement and productivity by optimizing the balance of security and convenience.

identity provider (IdP)

An identity provider stores and manages identity information about users. It authenticates these users for other service providers within one or more domains. Identity Services Auth Server is an IdP. It enables you to integrate federated user stores and create authentication flows for multiple separate realms, each of which has its own configuration.

identity token

A token that provides identity information about the user. Part of the OpenID Connect specification.

Infrastructure and Platform Services (IPS)

A collection of services that provide a secure infrastructure that connect all Backbase services. IPS includes the Edge Service that routes requests to all backend micro-services, the Registry Service to control which services are available and how they can be contacted, and the Authentication Service to validate user access.

interaction

Any technology necessary for defining or rendering complex user interaction flows.

interaction flow

The flow that is needed to communicate with the web user or office employee, such as when requesting more information.

interest compensation

A mechanism that allows debit balances to be compensated with credit balances over a certain period when calculating the interest, optimizing the interest to be settled with the customer.

interest payment scheme

A scheme that specifies the due dates on which accrued interest amounts are to be paid by the customer.

interest rate

The annualized cost of credit or debt capital calculated as percentage ratio of interest to the principal.

interest receivable

An item under current assets that represents the amount of interest that was reported as earned but has yet to be received as of the date of the balance sheet.

interest refixing period

See interest repricing period.

interest renewal

The process that manages the determination of new interest conditions to be applied to a product when the interest repricing period has ended.

interest repricing period

The agreed term during which the charged and earned percentage on the product do not change. When the term expires, a new rate is offered.

interest scheme

A scheme that specifies interest conditions, such as interest term, interest payment scheme, calculation base, day count convention, interest tiers, and others that apply for a product of the bank or a specific instance of a product, such as a savings agreement with a specific customer.

interest plan

See interest scheme.

intermediary bank

Bank acting on behalf of the receiving bank usually in the context of international transfers. For example, if the receiving bank is not able to receive an international transfer, the intermediary bank receives the payment order and transfers it to the receiving bank with a domestic payment. See also correspondent bank.

investment profile

A set of rules that defines how the influx of funds has to be spread over the assets, such as stocks, funds, deposits, and other financial investment instruments that need to be acquired.

investor profile

A set of an individual’s preferences in investment decisions, for example:

  • Short-term trading (active management) or long-term holding (buy and hold).

  • Risk-averse, risk-tolerant or risk-seeking.

  • All classes of assets or just one (for example, stocks).

investor style

See investor profile.

invoice

A request received from a supplier to pay for services or products delivered or to be delivered.

know your customer (KYC)

The process of verifying the identity of customers. In the financial context, this is taken further to comply with the governance regulations. This involves, among others, the obligation to classify the knowledge and experience of the customer related to the products that the bank wants to sell to such customer.

job role

A set of business function privileges grouped together. Users assigned to a job role assume the corresponding privileges.

legal entity

Any personal or non-personal entity that is involved within a transaction or arrangement with a bank. A legal entity that has an owner or joint-owner arrangement relationship is considered to be a customer of a bank.

lending

The process of providing funds temporarily, on condition that the amount borrowed be returned, usually with an interest fee.

limits per payment function

The constraints applied to interactions such as single transactions and transactions over a specific period. Limits can be applied to a function or a privilege. You can only define limits for financial transactions such as payments. For example, different limits for domestic and international transfers.

letter of credit

A document from a bank guaranteeing that the seller will receive payment in full as long as certain delivery conditions have been met. In the event that the buyer is unable to make payment on the purchase, the bank covers the outstanding amount. Since both the buyer and the seller are involved, there can be distinguished two types of letters of credit from the customer’s point of view:

  • Letters of credit issued on behalf of the customer, where the customer is the buyer.

  • Letters of credit received by the customer’s bank, where the customer is the seller.

letter of guarantee

A document from a bank guaranteeing that a seller will receive payment in full in case the buyer does not fulfill the stipulated obligations under the contract. Since both the buyer and the seller are involved, there can be distinguished two types of letters of credit from the customer’s point of view:

  • Letters of guarantee issued on behalf of the customer, where the customer is the buyer.

  • Letters of guarantee received by the customer’s bank, where the customer is the seller.

library package

A node module that can be imported by another node module, as in import x from '@my-company/my-package'. Library packages can contain one or more widgets or containers, or they can be Javascript libraries. A package can be either public or private.

line of credit

See credit limit.

loan

An instance of a lending contract or agreement, under which money is provided temporarily to a customer on condition that the amount borrowed is returned, usually with an interest fee.

loan continuation

The process whereby the customer gets a proposal to proceed the loan within the context of an agreement with the bank, usually against actualized conditions.

loan premature (partial) redemption

The process whereby the loan or its part is paid back before the date initially agreed (maturity date). Usually the bank charges a penalty if the agreed interest is higher than the current market rate for the respective term.

mandate

The authority to execute a financial task up to a specified limit; in the context of direct debits, pre-approval for one or more collections.

maturity date

The formally agreed date when a financial agreement or contract ends.

master page

A specialized page that can be used as a template for creating pages in an experience. Master pages are stored in the Enterprise Catalog and can be added to an experience’s Experience Catalog.

member

A person, country, or organization that has joined a group, society, or team. Within Backbase, this term is mostly used in the context of US Credit Unions where US law requires that only members have access to the Credit Unions and their services.

Mobile SDK

A software development kit that enables developers to create hybrid or fully native mobile apps for iOS and Android. This SDK is the core component of our mobile set of tools and is targeted to empower new and existing mobile apps with typical Backbase-specific features. Amongst others, it provides features such as navigation, content targeting, and security.

modeller

A Flow utility that allows users to build BPMN, CMMN, and DMN models.

money market

Market in which money is borrowed and lent through the sale and purchase of short-term debt instruments, normally of up to 12-month maturity, such as certificates of deposit, commercial papers, treasury bills, banker’s acceptances, and repurchase agreements. Includes interbank market.

money market deposit placed

Money borrowed to a counterparty on the money market.

money market deposit taken

Money borrowed from a counterparty on the money market.

money market deposits

Money held on the money market rather than in bank accounts.

money movement

A generic term used to describe the transfer of funds from one account to another. This comprises all kinds of movements, such as:

  • Domestic or Payment area transfers (such as SEPA CT, wire transfers, A2A, P2P, BACS, CHAPS, Faster Payments)

  • International transfers

  • Bill payments

  • Collections

mono-repository

A single repository hosting the source code of all Angular apps and library packages for a company.

multi-repository

A collection of repositories, with each repository hosting the source code of Angular apps and library packages.

multipackage-repository

A repository hosting the source code for multiple library packages.

native mobile client

Digital banking widgets and custom user interfaces interact with DBS through clients by using native APIs. A native mobile client mainly serves as a wrapper of various REST interfaces, performs data aggregation and transformation, and can contain additional logic. These clients are distributed as closed-source Android libraries and iOS frameworks and can be used in mobile applications.

notification

In the context of the Actions capability, an update sent to bank customers in response to specific business events, via one of the defined communication channels. This can be via email, in-app DBS Notifications, or SMS. Notifications can be of the following types:

  • Real-time notifications - Actions sends a notification via the specified channel every time a corresponding business event occurs. For example, a bank customer receives a text message whenever a new transaction is made on their current account. Real-time notifications are the default option.

  • Digest notifications - Actions accumulates all occurrences of business events over the previous period and delivers a single digest notification at the scheduled time. For example, at 9 AM every Monday a bank customer receives an email with all transactions made on their current account over the previous week.

notification template

A blueprint of the notifications sent to bank customers by Actions. Templates can include static and dynamic content.

notional amount

The nominal or face amount value of a financial instrument.

notional cash pooling

A mechanism for calculating interest on the combined credit and debit balances of accounts that a corporate parent chooses to cluster together, without actually transferring any funds between the accounts involved. It can also refer to virtual cash concentration where the accounts stay as and where they are, but are represented by the bank as if they were centralized in one account.

notional principal amount

See notional amount.

notional value

See notional amount.

offset account

An account linked to a loan in such a way that the balance of the respective account is used on a daily basis to calculate the interest due, effectively lowering the amount owed on the loan. The term can also refer to the right of the bank to seize a debtor’s account balance in case of the debt being in default.

open date

The date when an agreement, contract, or arrangement becomes or became effectively active.

overdraft

A deficit on an account caused by either drawing more money than the account holds, or exceeding an agreed credit limit on that account.

overdraft facility

An agreement between a bank and a customer under which the amount of the facility is the amount that the customer is allowed to overdraw on the related current or checking account.

page

The main entry point to an experience. When a page lives in a catalog, it is called a master page.

party

An entity that takes part or is involved in an agreement or transaction. This is used as a generic term referring to entities playing different roles (for example, customer, beneficiary, orderer, accountant, notary, etc.) in the interaction with a bank.

payables

See accounts payable.

payment

See payment order.

payment order

An instruction to credit or debit amount of money to an account. A payment order is initiated by the initiation party - a debtor, a creditor, or a party that acts on their behalf.

payment order batch

A group of payment orders that is to be processed together.

Payveris

A cloud-based digital payments and money movement platform for financial institutions and their digital partners. Payveris integration comes out of the box with the Bill Pay capability.

Payee group

A grouping mechanism for payees. Payee groups contain one or more payees defined in the context of a service agreement. The payee group manages access for making payments to these payees. You can include one payee in multiple payee groups.

permission

A combination of a business function and privileges that is assigned to a user, for example, manage contacts or perform intracompany payments. Additionally this can be limited to specific resources.

personal finance management (PFM)

Software that helps users manage their money. PFM often lets users categorize transactions and add accounts from multiple institutions into a single view. PFM also typically includes data visualizations such as spending trends, budgets, and net worth.

physical cash concentration

A mechanism of periodically (for example, at the end of each day) transferring funds from different corporate accounts to one designated account.

pipeline

A continuous delivery (CD) pipeline is an automated expression of your process of getting software from version control right through to your users and customers. Every change to your software committed in source control goes through a complex process on its way to being released. This process involves building the software in a reliable and repeatable manner.

portfolio

A collection of financial assets such as stocks, bonds, and cash.

posting

A debit or credit booking against an account, which respectively decreases or increases the balance of the account.

premature repayment

See loan premature redemption.

prepaid card

Plastic or virtual card issued by a bank that customers use to buy goods and services based on an amount that is already posted to the related card account. The issuer pays for the product based on the amount prepaid by the consumer.

principal

See principal amount.

principal amount

The amount that was originally contracted for the respective product. This is mainly used in the context of loans and deposits. This differs from the booked balance, since principal amount can and for most loans will change over time.

prior repayment

See loan premature redemption.

private package

A library package that is only used by apps within the same repository, or other private library packages in the same repository. These are the libraries that do not have a package.json, or they have private: true in their package.json.

privilege

A specific action that a user is allowed to perform on a specific business function, including approve, create, delete, edit, view, and cancel.

The flow for a BPMN Process or a Case Management process.

product

A financial instrument, such as a credit or debit card, a business current account, or a 25-year fixed-rate mortgage. A product holds reference data such as the date and channel availability, or an icon representing this product in your app.

product group

A library package in Angular Package Format package and released to be consumed by packages or apps outside of the repository as an NPM dependency.

RESTful API Modeling Language. A YAML-based language to describe RESTful APIs. It is used in Backbase to describe a single capability.

A repository for hosting the RAML files for creating data packages.

realm

A space that manages a set of users, credentials, roles, and groups. A user belongs to and logs into a realm. Realms are isolated from one another and can only manage and authenticate the users that they control.

receivables

See accounts receivable.

recurring payment

A recurring payment is a form of collection where a bank account holder authorizes a third party to charge money from their debit or credit card.

Related terms are: standing order and direct debit.

redemption plan

See redemption scheme.

redemption scheme

A scheme that specifies due dates on which amounts are to be repaid by the customer.

repayment schedule

See redemption scheme.

repository

A storage location hosting the source code for generated and deployable artifacts.

An action that a user must perform during the authentication process. A user can not complete the authentication process until these actions are performed. reservation

Blocking a specific amount on an account.

A Flow utility that provides a set of responsive widgets, service integration accelerators, BPMN models, and DMN models that are focused on allowing existing clients to buy a bank product such as credit card or mortgage.

role

A characteristic that identifies a type or category of user. Admin, user, manager, and employee are all typical roles that may exist in an organization. Applications often assign access and permissions to specific roles rather than individual users as dealing with users can be too fine grained and hard to manage.

safe-to-spend balance

Balance of an account subtracting upcoming bill payments, pending transactions, and any configured saving goals.

savings

Money that is not spent, usually kept on an account that pays interest.

savings account

An interest-bearing bank account that pays interest. Generally, checks are not written on a savings account, but funds can be withdrawn on demand.

saving goal

A preset target that is to be reached. The target usually includes a desired amount and a target date when the target should be achieved.

secured loan

An amount lent by the bank to a customer that is backed by a collateral. If the customer defaults, the collateral or part of it can be claimed by the bank.

segregation of responsibilities

See four eyes principle.

service account

Each client has a built-in service account which allows it to obtain an access token.

service agreement

A contract that includes one or more legal entities. A legal entity that participates in a service agreement can allow a subset of its users to act through the corresponding service agreement and/or allow a subset of its arrangements to be accessed through the service agreement. Within each service agreement, permissions to perform specific tasks are granted to users, including access to data across one or more legal entities participating in the corresponding service agreement. As such, a service agreement is a way to give third-party users specific access to arrangements.

service integration accelerator

A Flow utility that provides accelerators, via the Process Management capability, that developers can use to integrate third-party services into their processes.

Service SDK

A software development kit that empowers developers to kickstart new projects, offering best practices, tools and libraries. The robust and opinionated archetypes are used to quickly build banking services. Service SDK combines sets of ready-to-go libraries to scaffold different service types such as presentation, persistence, and integration.

session

A temporary interchange between a user and a system. When a user logs in, a session is created to manage the login session. A session contains information like when the user logged in and what applications have participated within single sign-on during that session. Both admins and users can view session information.

setoff account

See offset account.

settlement account

An account that is to be used to pay an amount of money owed in a specific context. For example, this can include the payment of debit interest or charges due.

signing mandate

The limit up to which a user is allowed to approve or co-approve a specific transaction by means of a (digital) signature.

standing order

A standing order is an instruction a bank account holder gives to their bank to pay a set amount at regular intervals to another’s account (source: Wikipedia). An example of a standing order is when a house owner has set up an automatic monthly payment of their mortgage installment.

Related terms are: recurring payment and direct debit.

standing payment instruction

See standing order.

sweep account

A financial product explicitly set up to support the management of funds between accounts in order to optimize capital yields.

sweeping

See account sweeping.

system job role

A special kind of job role that is automatically assigned to service agreement Administrators.

If you need to have a User who can perform administrative tasks (e.g. manage Users and Permissions, manage Job Roles, etc.), you can promote them as a service agreement Admin. Once it is done, a predefined set of entitlements-related permissions (defined in the assignable permission set for Admin users) is automatically generated and assigned to User’s Permissions.

The system Job role would be effective even for a disabled SA - in this case Admins would be able to login into Service agreement context and perform their tasks with given permissions.

system recipe

An action recipe defined by a bank developer. Such recipes may be used to define actions that must be performed for all users and that cannot be disabled, enabled, or removed by the bank user. System recipes can also be used to deliver some business-banking functionality (for example, an e-mail can be sent to the frauds department whenever a transaction exceeds a certain threshold).

task

An activity or a business function that the user can perform using the system. For example, create a new contact entry or enter an international transfer.

An activity that needs to be performed. If it should be performed by a human, it is a manual task; if the task should be performed by a computer, it is a service task or a decision task.

term deposit

Deposit held in a bank that pays or charges a stated interest rate for a fixed period, as long as the customer does not withdraw the money early.

themes

A predefined combination of HTML templates and stylesheets. Every screen provided by Auth Server is backed by a theme.

third party

A legal entity that is not a party to a contract or a transaction, but has a certain involvement in it. For example, an accountant that has view-only access to accounts and transactions of a bank customer in order to produce some financial reports.

time deposit

See term deposit.

trade finance

A business domain that intermediates in the handling of financial transactions between sellers and buyers resulting from domestic and international trades. A customer of a bank can be either the buyer or the seller.

transaction

A payment order or any other financial process that is executed on an account.

transfer

A specific type of payment order.

transfer type

A subdivision of transfers.

two-man rule

See four eyes principle.

two-person rule

See four eyes principle.

UI component

an Angular component that does not have services injected, only inputs and outputs decorated with @Input and @Output. UI components are always children of container components.

unified money movement

The concept whereby the user does not have to pre-select a specific type of payment. The payment type is derived from the contextual information entered in the payment request. For example, when an email address is used, a P2P payment is initiated.

Universal Widget Collection

A selection of widgets and containers that are used to define the navigation and layout of an experience. Available in two formats, Angular and Handlebars, this collection enables users to easily build user-friendly experiences.

unsecured loan

An amount lent by the bank to a customer that is not backed by a collateral.

user

Any person that interacts with the bank using banking applications on behalf of themselves or another entity they are representing. Representation is typically applicable for corporate customers. A user can have associated attributes such as email, username, address, phone number, and birth date. A user can be assigned group membership and have a specific role assigned.

user data management

A service that provides a persistent, unified user database. This is required for Flow capabilities such as the Retail Onboarding Journey.

user profile

usufruct

The right to use or get the yield on a product that is owned by another party.

Balance of an account on a specific date that is used for calculation of interest. Unlike the booked balance, a backdated value date balance can change due to postdated transactions.

The date when an amount posted to an account becomes interest bearing.

vendor package

verification

The process of checking the data, which was fed to the system by a user or process, for completeness and correctness by the current or another user.
