Understand Payments

A payment is an international banking term that refers to a directive to a bank or other financial institution from a bank account holder instructing the bank to make a payment or series of payments to a third party.

Payments in Backbase Platform allow the user to transfer money from one account to another. Users either select the account to transfer to from their contact list, their own accounts, or they specify new account data.

Users can schedule payments for one-time execution or recur during a defined period or for a defined number of times. Administrators control whether standing orders are possible with a preference on the widget.

Backbase Platform always works in conjunction with a core banking system for the actual processing of a transaction. The widget that initiates the payment sends the payment order to the backend, which is either Backbase’s own DBS or a third-party system. The payment order ends up in the core banking system that processes the payment order and, if successful, sends the payment to the beneficiary.

Note
 In addition to Payments, Backbase offers these other money transfer capabilities: Bill Pay for bank users to pay their electronic or paper bills, and Remote Deposit Capture (RDC) that allows bank users to scan and deposit a check using their mobile phone camera.

Initiate SEPA payment

Single Euro Payments Area (SEPA) is an effort of the European Union to harmonize euro payments between banks. A credit transfer is an electronic payment from one bank account to another. The SEPA Credit Transfer (SCT) scheme makes the transfer of money in Europe easy and convenient (source: European Payments Council).

Users initiate a SEPA credit transfer from their account to any other SEPA reachable account.

Initiate UK payment

For UK customers, supported payment types include the following:

  • Faster Payments: Suited for smaller transactions with a £250k maximum. The paying and the receiving banks must be direct or indirect members of the service. Payments are processed almost instantly or at least within two hours. Banks usually charge no fee. Read more on the Faster Payments website.

  • CHAPS: Clearing House Automated Payment System, or CHAPS, is used to make payments that need to be processed on the same working day. Because the transfer fee that banks charge for a CHAPS payment is relatively high, Faster Payments is the payment system used for most payments and CHAPS is used for high-value transactions above £250k.

  • BACS: Bankers' Automated Clearing System, or BACS, is used for recurring transfers, such as utility bills, mortgages, and subscriptions. These payments are usually Direct Debits or Bacs Direct Credits, and they are processed within three working days.

Initiate US payment

The United States have several electronic payment methods, such as a US domestic wire transfer and ACH payments.

Retail Banking covers the following payment method:

  • A2A external transfer: Payment between the user’s own accounts at different financial institutions

About A2A external transfer:

Account-to-account external transfers are quick and easy money transfers between accounts that the user has access to at different banks.

Backbase Platform supports connecting a US account, at a different bank, that the user has access to. Users need to activate connected accounts before being able to use it for A2A transfers. The purpose of the activation is to verify that the user has access to the account.

With the connected and activated account in place, the user can view the external account list, select an external account to view its details, and initiate a payment to or withdraw, that is, pull funds from the external account.

Initiate international transfer

International transfers are payments to another country.

Because the required payment details differ per country, the widget requests the expected payment details from the backend and flexibly adjusts the input controls and validations based on the response.

For example, the customer wants to make a payment from a US account to an EU account. The widget requests the expected payment details from the backend. The backend returns that the allowed currencies are US Dollar and Euro and the expected account number format is IBAN. The widget uses this information to assemble the next screen where the customer enters the necessary payment details and creates the payment.

Internal transfer

Internal transfer is the transfer between the accounts of the same bank customer in the same financial institution. This includes transfers between different types of accounts, for example, from a loan account to a credit account, and others.

Manage payment orders

The initiate payment functionalities support scheduling a payment to be executed in the future, either as a one-off or as a standing order. The manage payment orders functionality lists these future payment orders and allows users to manage them.
