Standard support terms

Backbase software versions and releases are supported according to the following schedule:

  • Release: A release is supported for a period of 24 months after the commercial release of the next release, but for no longer than 3 years. To be eligible for support, customers must use the most recent major version and minor version of a release.

  • Major version: A major version will be supported for a period of 12 months after the commercial release of the next major version. To be eligible for support, customers must use the most recent minor version of the major version.

  • Minor version: A minor version will be supported for a period of 2 months after the commercial release of the next minor version.

  • Sample code: Sample code and any versions and releases that are identified by Backbase as developmental, beta, or pre-release are not supported.

To keep software eligible for support, customers must install patches, new versions, and releases as required by Backbase. Backbase does not support software that does not meet these terms.

Note
 These support terms may vary on a customer-by-customer basis. Always refer to your M&S agreement for the applicable terms.

The following table provides some examples:

Deprecations

To mitigate the impact of deprecations, Backbase provides advance notice in the release notes. Deprecations are announced at least one full major version ahead of changes.

Note
 Deprecations include any updates that will cause breaking changes in a future major release. Until then, deprecated features, configuration, and related functionality will continue to work and remain fully supported. The release notes list deprecations to allow sufficient time to plan and update your deployment or implementation before the breaking changes are introduced.
